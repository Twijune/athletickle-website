Most fitness apps treat your body as a data pipeline. Every set you log, every workout you finish, every metric you enter gets shipped to a server, cross-referenced, and — often enough — monetized. We built Athletickle the other way around, on purpose.

## The Default Is Surveillance

The typical training app wants an account before you can do anything. That account exists so your data can live on someone else's server, tied to your identity, forever. Once it's there you're exposed to everything that comes with it: breaches, quiet policy changes, "anonymized" datasets sold to third parties, and an ad profile you never asked for.

Health and fitness data is some of the most sensitive data there is. It shouldn't be the price of admission to a workout tracker.

## Your Training Data Stays on Your Device

Athletickle runs entirely on your phone. The programs it builds, the sets you log, your history and settings — all of it is created and stored locally, on your device. We operate no server that receives it. We can't read it, we can't sell it, and we can't lose it in a breach, because we never have it in the first place.

The algorithm that adapts your training runs on-device too. Your data doesn't need to leave your phone for the app to do its job.

## Cookieless Analytics on the Website

We do want to know roughly how many people visit this website and where they find us — that's it. So the site uses [Glyphex](https://glyphex.io), a privacy-first analytics tool, and nothing else:

- No cookies, so there's no annoying consent banner to click through.
- No fingerprinting and no long-term tracking — visitor identifiers are anonymous and reset every day.
- Your IP address is used for a moment to derive an approximate location, then discarded. It's never stored.

That gives us honest, aggregate traffic numbers without building a profile of you.

## No Accounts, No Middleman Payments

There's nothing to sign up for. Subscriptions run through the Apple App Store and Google Play, so your payment details are handled by them under their own policies — we never see them. No account database means nothing to leak.

## Why This Is Better for You

Privacy-first isn't just a nice principle; it's a better deal:

- **Nothing to breach.** Data we never collect can't be stolen from us.
- **No profile.** You aren't the product, and there's no ad targeting built on your training.
- **It just works offline.** Because the app doesn't depend on a server, your training goes with you.

We'll be honest about the tradeoff: keeping everything on-device means we don't offer cloud backup or cross-device sync yet. If we ever add it, it will be opt-in, clearly explained, and designed to keep as much on your device as possible — not a reason to start hoovering up your data.
